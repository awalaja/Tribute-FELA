Times in the life of Fela Anikulapo Kuti

  • 1938 Born in Abeokuta, north of Lagos Nigeria
  • 1958 Parents sent him off to London for what they assumed would be a medical education; instead, Fela registered at Trinity College's school of music
  • 1960 Married his first wife, Remilekun (Remi) Taylor, with whom he would have three children (Femi, Yeni, and Sola).
  • 1961 Fela formed his first band, Koola Lobitos, in 1961, and quickly became a fixture on the London club scene.
  • 1963 He returned to Nigeria and started another version of Koola Lobitos that was more influenced by the James Brown-style singing of Geraldo Pina from Sierra Leone.
  • 1969 He returned to Nigeria in 1963 and started another version of Koola Lobitos that was more influenced by the James Brown-style singing of Geraldo Pina from Sierra Leone.
  • 1977 In one of the most egregious acts of violence committed against him, 1,000 Nigerian soldiers attacked his Kalakuta compound.
  • 1978 Returned to Nigeria from exile in Ghana after the Kalakuta tragedy
  • 1984 Fela was sentenced to ten years in prison on charges of currency smuggling.
  • 1985 Freed from prison with help from Amnesty International.
  • 1997 Died aged 49.
